eBillity Timesheet

Log and manage your TimeTracker hours by just talking to Claude

v1.0.0
What this does. This skill fills out your eBillity (TimeTracker) weekly timesheet for you. Tell Claude what you worked on in plain language — "I had a meeting with ampi for an hour yesterday" — and it finds the job, picks the service item, and enters the hours, leaving everything in Pending so nothing is committed until you say so. It can also answer questions about your time, check whether your week is ready, and fix entries a manager sent back.

Two things it will never do: type your eBillity password (you sign in, it takes over after) and submit or approve time on its own. This is a one-time setup. After it, you just talk to Claude.

How you actually use it (just describe the job)

  • Log 4 hours on AMP-048 for yesterday.
  • I had a meeting with ampi this morning for an hour — put it on the cheddar label job.
  • Add two more hours to AMP-049 on Tuesday.
  • How many hours do I have on AMP-034 this week?
  • Is my week ready to submit?
  • Submit my timesheet. (It shows you a summary and confirms first.)

What's inside

The actual files in this skill, exactly what's in the download.

Files
SKILL.md
1
Get the skill file

You need ebillity-timesheet.skill. Download it from the catalog and save it somewhere easy to find (Downloads is fine).

Note: If your file picker won't accept a .skill file, rename it to ebillity-timesheet.skill.zip to upload, then rename it back afterward if needed.
2
Upload the skill to Claude

In Claude, go to Settings → Claude Cowork → Upload skill and pick your ebillity-timesheet.skill file. You'll then see eBillity Timesheet in your skills list.

Invoke it with /ebillity-timesheet or just by describing what you want ("log 3 hours on AMP-048 today"). No version number needed.
3
Connect Claude to your Chrome

This skill works by driving your web browser — it opens the eBillity timesheet and fills it the way you would. It doesn't run code or install anything on your Mac.

So you need Claude in Chrome connected: install the Claude browser extension and connect the Chrome you use for eBillity. When the skill runs, Claude uses that browser.

Note: If more than one Chrome is connected, the skill asks which to use. Pick the one signed into eBillity.
4
Sign into eBillity yourself

In that Chrome, log into eBillity (TimeTracker) as you normally would. The skill never types your password — if it hits a login screen it pauses and hands it to you, then continues once you're in.

Stay signed in while you work. If your session times out mid-task, the skill notices, won't pretend it saved, and asks you to sign back in before redoing the step.

5
Try it — read first, then log

Start with a question, which changes nothing:

"What's pending on my timesheet this week?"

Then log a real entry:

"Log 2 hours on AMP-048 for yesterday."

Claude finds the job, sets the service item, enters the hours, and you'll see eBillity's green "Saved successfully" confirmation. It stays Pending — nothing is submitted.

You're set. From here just describe your time: "add an hour to the dinner bell job Thursday," "how many hours this week," "is my week ready," "submit my timesheet." The skill handles the rest.

Troubleshooting

It stopped at a login screen, or says I'm not signed in
By design — the skill never enters your eBillity password. Sign in yourself in the connected Chrome, then tell Claude to continue. If a session dropped mid-task, it catches that and won't report a false save; just sign back in and it picks up.
It logged time to the wrong project
A client name like "ampi" can match several jobs (AMP-048, AMP-049, and more). Give the job number, or pick from the list it shows. Everything is Pending, so fixing or clearing it takes a second.
It picked the wrong service item
Just name it — Design, Account Management, Copywriting, Art Buy, and so on. The skill matches against your real eBillity menu and won't invent one. You can also say "set the service item to Design" after the fact.
I don't want it to submit or approve anything
It won't. Logging only ever leaves time Pending. It submits or approves only when you explicitly say "submit my timesheet" or "approve all," and it shows a summary and asks first.
It didn't seem to save
eBillity autosaves and flashes a green "Saved successfully" banner; the skill checks for that before reporting success. If nothing saved (usually a dropped session), sign back in and ask it to redo the entry.
How do I undo what it just entered?
Right after, say "scratch that" or "make it 3 instead" and it corrects the last action. Otherwise the entry is Pending, so you can have it clear the cell anytime before you submit.
Can it do expenses, or approve my team's time?
No — on purpose. This is a personal time tool only. It doesn't touch expenses or notes, and it won't approve other people's time or lock time periods. Those stay out of this skill.
