1
Get the skill file

You need capsule-brand.skill. Download it from the catalog and save it somewhere easy to find (Downloads is fine).

Note: If your file picker won't accept a .skill file, rename it to capsule-brand.skill.zip before uploading, then rename it back if you need to.
2
Upload the skill to Claude

In Claude.ai, go to Settings → Claude Cowork → Upload skill, then pick your capsule-brand.skill file.

Once uploaded, you'll see Capsule Brand listed under your skills. That's it, the skill is now available in any Cowork session.

It's invoked as /capsule-brand or just by describing what you want. You don't need to type a version number.
3
Give Claude what it needs

For most jobs, nothing. The palette (with Pantone and CMYK builds), the type rules, the deck structure and the voice guidance are all inside the skill, and the six logo SVGs ride along in assets/logos/. You already have Britanica, GT Super and Proxima Nova installed, so type renders correctly out of the box.

Point it at a folder only when the job needs a texture or the 25th anniversary badge. Those live in Dropbox at:

Design Resources/ >> Logo Collection <</Capsule/Claude - Design System/

That folder name really does start with a space before the >>. If you paste the path and it fails, that's why.
4
Use it — just ask

You don't invoke it. Once the skill is installed it fires on its own whenever you're making something that goes out under our name. Describe the job in plain words and it applies the palette, the type rules, the deck structure and the voice without being told to.

One thing genuinely worth doing: say where the work is going. Type resolves differently by destination — Illustrator and Keynote get Britanica and GT Super, Google Slides gets Instrument Sans and Gilda Display, because Slides can't render our licensed fonts. Mention the destination and it picks correctly. Leave it out and it has to guess.

Checking work — often the most useful thing it does

Is this deck on-brand? Here's the PDF.
Check these colours against our palette and tell me what's off.
This headline is set in Britanica Bold — what's the Instrument Sans equivalent?
Rewrite this paragraph in our voice.

Two things it already knows, so you don't have to say them. Our supplied logo files carry two older reds that don't match PMS 186 C — it corrects them automatically. And on client work, the client's brand governs and ours stays on the cover; say who the work is for and it keeps them separate.

It's better at being corrected than at guessing. If something comes out wrong, say what's wrong — "the type's too heavy", "that grey isn't ours" — and it'll fix it and remember for the rest of the conversation.

Troubleshooting

The type looks generic / everything came out in Helvetica
Almost always means Britanica or GT Super isn't active on your machine — check Font Book. Everyone here should have them already, but if you're new or on a fresh laptop, the files are in Dropbox under Claude - Design System/Fonts/. Nothing errors when they're missing; the work just quietly stops looking like ours.
I can't find that Dropbox folder
The folder name begins with a space before the >> — a pasted path without it will fail. If Design Resources isn't syncing at all, check Dropbox selective sync, and ask Brian if it isn't shared with you.
Which red is the right red?
PMS 186 C / #C4242B. Always, no exceptions. The logo files inside this skill are already correct. But two older reds — #b90021 and #d61f26 — are still sitting in the master SVGs in the Logo Collection folder. If you grab a logo from Dropbox instead of from the skill, check the fill and fix it to #C4242B first.
I'm making something for a client, not for Capsule
Then the client's brand governs the work and this skill should stay out of it — use it only for the wrapper (cover, credentials, contact). Say so up front and Claude will keep them separate.
Claude didn't seem to use the skill
Make sure it's listed under Settings → Claude Cowork. Then either describe the job clearly or type /capsule-brand to invoke it directly.
The result isn't quite what I wanted
Tell Claude what to change in plain words and it will iterate. The more specific the context you give it up front, the closer the first pass lands.
